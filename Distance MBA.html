NMIMS Distance MBA Admission Process


To register for NMIMS Distance MBA program, the students have to fill the registration form and pay the admission processing fee. For the same, the students have to visit the Authorised Enrollment Partner Office.

Documents required for the documentation process are as follows:

  • ● Academic documents
  • ● Identity proof like Aadhar Card, Voter Id or any other Govt issued ID proof
  • ● Documents for work experience (if any)
  • ● Passport size photographs

All the documents will be assessed by the counsellor at the Authorised Enrollment Partner’s office. After the authentication, students have to pay the Distance MBA fees. NMIMS will then verify all the documents and grant a student number to the student. applicant.

What is NMIMS Distance MBA program Fee structure?

NMIMS offers an affordable Distance MBA degree with a variety of options available for students to pay their respective fees.

The updated NMIMS distance MBA fee structure is: INR 1,68,000 Pro MBA Course INR 1,44,000 Prime MBA Course

There are 3 options to make NMIMS distance MBA fee:

  • Full Fee Payment:Students have the option to pay the whole fee amount in one time.
  • Pay yearly: Students can pay the MBA course fee annually with a payment of INR 91,000 (Pro course) and INR 79,000 (Prime) every year.
  • Semester-wise payment:Students can opt for this option for convenient fee payments of INR 48,000 (Pro course) and 42,000 (Prime) per semester.

The University offers special incentives to our armed forces with a 20% concession on the program fee.

  • Pro: The Pro mode allows students to access live-interactive lectures as well as view recorded lectures
  • Prime:The Prime delivery mode allows students to view recorded lectures
  • Prime +:The Prime+ mode allows students to choose access to live-interactive lectures of their choice.

How are exams conducted for distance MBA in NMIMS?

The maximum marks for TEE (Distance/ Diploma/ Certificate) is 70 marks and the exam duration is 2.5 hours. Out of the total 70 marks, 50 marks are for multiple choice questions and the remaining 20 marks are for two descriptive questions worth 10 marks each. In the MCQ section, there are 34 MCQ-type questions worth 0.5 marks each. Other than that, there are 25 questions worth 1 mark each and 4 questions worth 2 marks each. The students have to pay an exam fee, that is, INR 600/-.
