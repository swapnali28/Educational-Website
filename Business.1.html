MBA Courses List and Details

February 03, 2023

Do You ever wonder which Mba course would be good for your career? If you are confused between various Mba streams ten this article will help you in choosing the best course for you. This article will help you choose the best course for you.

The following is a list of MBA specialties available in India:

1. Master of Business Administration in Marketing

A marketer’s job is to understand and discover the wants of customers in order to provide them with a product or service that meets those demands. They must also compete with and outperform competitors in the same market. A marketer must also make the most of the marketing resources available to him.

In keeping with traditional marketing, an MBA in Marketing concentrates around the 4P’s – Product, Price, Place, and Promotion. Understanding customer behavior, creating relationships with them, and a little bit of Internet marketing are all covered in this MBA specialization.

Strategy development, analytical thinking, entrepreneurial abilities, and creativity are all required. The Indian Institute of Management (Ahmedabad), the Indian Institute of Management (Calcutta), and the Institute of Public Enterprise all offer MBA programs in marketing (Hyderabad)

MBA Marketing Management: Course Highlights

Course Level Post Graduate
Eligibility Bachelor’s degree in any discipline with a minimum of 50% aggregate marks
Entrance Exams CAT,MAT,GMAT,CMAT,XAT,NMAT
Admission Process Entrance Exams followed by Group Discussions, Written Analysis tests, and Personal Interviews.
Course Fee INR 3,00,000 – 10,00,000

2. Digital Marketing MBA

We’re all aware of the growing relevance of digital marketing, which is why several universities have begun to offer an MBA in Digital Marketing as a complement to traditional marketing abilities. This is a relatively new addition to the MBA specializations, but it is also one of the most popular.

An MBA in Digital Marketing gives you a wide range of digital capabilities, including SEO, SEM, SMM, website development, content management, email marketing, and more, as well as managerial abilities.

In just 11 months, IIDE provides an MBA-level Post Graduation in Digital Marketing that includes all of the above, as well as capstone projects, a mandatory 2-month internship, and guaranteed placement support.

Strategic thinking, multitasking, analytic approach, and communication abilities are all required.

Digital Marketing Course Details

Course Digital Marketing
Duration 3 Months
Entrance Exams CAT,MAT,GMAT,CMAT,XAT,NMAT
Fee Offered Rs. 36,000
Course Type Certified Exam
Starting salary offered Rs. 12,000 to 15,000 per month

3. Business Administration Master’s Degree

Top multinational corporations have set up shop in India as a result of globalization, increasing the demand for business specialists. As a result, an MBA in Company Management educates you in all facets of business, including human resources, finance, marketing, operations, retail, and more.

This MBA specialization prepares you to be an all-rounder and is also a good choice for people who wish to run their own company or start one in the future.

Entrepreneurial talents, teamwork, leadership traits, and crisis management skills are all required.

Indian Institute of Management (Ahmedabad, Bangalore, Calcutta, Lucknow), Symbiosis Centre for Management and Human Resources Development (Ahmedabad, Bangalore, Calcutta, Lucknow), Symbiosis Centre for Management and Human Resources Development (Ahmedabad, Bangalore, Calcutta, Lucknow), Symbiosis Centre for Management and Human Resources Development (A (Pune)

Many B-schools now offer dual specialties, allowing students to select between the two. According to the research of the leading B-schools, the total cost of an MBA program ranges from 10 lakhs to 20 lakhs. As a result, we may anticipate that the average fees will be around 15 lakhs.

IIM MBA Eligibility
  • Aspirants must have completed their 10+2 in any stream from a recognised board with a minimum aggregate of 60%.
  • Complete your education at a recognised university with a minimum of a 50% grade. For candidates who fall into the ST/SC categories, there will be a 5% reduction on the minimum score.
  • For international students, aspirants must pass tests such as the Common Admission Test and the Graduate Management Admission Test.
  • Shortlisted candidates may be asked to participate in a Group Discussion or personal interview.

4. Human Resources MBA

A company’s human resources department is in charge of hiring, training, and developing a team of personnel. They must keep track of their performance on a regular basis, manage their salaries and promotions, develop a strong employee policy to promote healthy working environments and look after their health and safety needs. An MBA in Human Resources will provide you with all of the essential information and training to carry out the obligations listed above.

Crisis management abilities, clear communication skills, leadership traits, sensitivity, and coordination skills are all required.

Tata Institute of Social Sciences (Mumbai), Management Development Institute (Delhi), and Xavier Labour Relations Institute are among the top institutions offering an MBA in HR (Jamshedpur)

5. Master of Business Administration in Finance

A finance manager’s job is to plan and control the company’s financial resources and assets. This also includes the function of analyzing and controlling financial risk. Accounting, money market work, banking, international finance, and privatization are all skills that an MBA in Finance may teach you.

Strong mathematical skills, strategic analytical thinking, leadership qualities, and knowledge of data metrics are all required.

SP Jain Institute of Management & Research (Mumbai), Jain University (Bangalore), IBMR Business School are some of the top institutions that provide an MBA in Finance (Gurgaon)

6. Operations Management MBA

An Operations Manager is responsible for the production and distribution of a company’s products and services. They must always seek new and better ways to improve the production, distribution, and cost of their goods and services.

An MBA in Operations Management provides you with the academic and practical understanding needed to carry out operations.

Coordination, problem-solving, and strategic thinking are all required talents.

Narayana Business School (Ahmedabad), DY Patil School University of Management (Mumbai), and Tapmi University of Management (Mumbai) are some of the top schools that provide an MBA in Operations Management (Manipal).

MBA in Operations Management Course Details.

Degree Masters
Minimum Percentage 50% marks in bachelors degree
Entrance Exams CAT,MAT,GMAT,CMAT,XAT,NMAT
Average Fees Incurred INR 3 – 27 Lakhs
Average Salary Offered INR 1.5 – 18 LPA

7. MBA in Healthcare Administration

The covid-19 pandemic has undoubtedly made us all understand that we require not just additional healthcare staff, but also a well-organized healthcare management system. All individuals interested in the fields of pharmaceuticals, insurance, and hospitals can pursue an MBA in Healthcare Management.

This MBA concentration trains you to manage the healthcare system, with a focus on how to respond in the event of a healthcare crisis.

Basic medical knowledge, adaptive thinking, ethical judgment, and honesty are all required skills.

Symbiosis Institute of Health Science (Pune), Indus University (Ahmedabad), and Manipal School of Management are among the top institutions offering an MBA in Healthcare Management (Manipal)

8. Information Technology MBA

If you’re interested in computer science, an MBA in Information Technology may be the right fit for you. This MBA prepares you to manage an organization’s information technology (IT), which encompasses all hardware, software, networks, and data centers.

You’ll be in charge of planning, designing, and implementing IT solutions for the company. Knowledge of IT in-depth, strategic planning, and global orientation are all required skills.

Jamnalal Bajaj Institute of Management Studies (Mumbai), BIMS (Calcutta), and Chandigarh University are among the top institutions offering an MBA in IT (Chandigarh)

9. Rural Management MBA

In response to the government’s altering socio-economic policies, a rising number of awake young learners are gravitating toward rural development. An MBA in Rural Management will teach you how to organize and manage agricultural activities.

Working closely with farmers and villagers would be a requirement of this MBA Specialization. Many institutes require students to participate in a mandatory internship during which they must live in rural communities and do research and studies.

Fluency in numerous local languages, mass communication abilities, and problem-solving skills are also required.

Welingkar Institute of Management (Mumbai), Amity School of Rural Management (Noida), and Indian Institute of Rural Management are among the top institutions offering an MBA in Rural Management (Jaipur)

MBA in Rural Management is a 2-year postgraduate course.

10. Logistics Management MBA

Under supply chain management, logistics management is a critical concept. The major responsibility of a logistics manager is to organize, implement, and control the arrival and outgoing of raw materials and completed goods for an organization’s production.

This MBA concentration also prepares you to develop new and effective logistics management strategies, lowering time and cost while raising production levels.

Calmness, problem-solving, and intelligent thinking are required skills.

Amity Business School (Noida), Presidency University (Bangalore), and VELS are some of the top schools that offer an MBA in Logistics Management (Chennai)

11. Event Management MBA

Event management has grown in popularity in recent years and is an excellent career choice. An MBA in Event Management teaches you how to identify and segment a specific target audience for an event, select themes that are relevant to them, plan logistics, promote the event extensively, provide an anticipated budget, and successfully manage the event.

Crisis management and heavy coordination are two significant features of event management. Without these two, events can devolve into pandemonium and the event manager’s worst nightmare.
